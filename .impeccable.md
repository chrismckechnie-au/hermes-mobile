## Design Context

### Audience

Hermes Mobile is for technical power users who operate one or more user-owned
Hermes Agent hosts from an Android phone. They often check long-running work
away from a desktop, switch between independent sessions, respond to
approvals, and intervene quickly when a run stalls or fails.

### Core jobs

- Start, monitor, interrupt, and resume independent Hermes sessions.
- Understand what Hermes is doing without reading noisy raw terminal output.
- See tasks, subagents, workspace changes, approvals, failures, and completion
  state at a glance.
- Manage hosts, models, reasoning, permissions, skills, tools, scheduled jobs,
  notifications, and the optional active-session overlay.

### Brand and tone

The interface should feel **calm, precise, and capable**: a refined
industrial/utilitarian command surface rather than a generic AI dashboard.
Hermes identity, restrained warm accents, dense-but-readable information, and
platform-native Android behavior should make the app feel trustworthy during
long-running or high-impact work.

### Product principles

1. **Work first.** Prioritize the current session, latest meaningful status,
   and actions that unblock work.
2. **Progressive disclosure.** Summarize tool traces, tasks, subagents, and
   diagnostics; reveal detail on demand rather than stacking permanent cards.
3. **Quiet status hierarchy.** Reserve strong color and interruption for
   approvals, failures, and completed outcomes. Routine activity should remain
   visible but unobtrusive.
4. **Touch-native and accessible.** Use predictable Android navigation,
   minimum 48 dp targets, semantic labels, dynamic text resilience, and
   immediate press feedback.

### Avoid

- Generic “AI dashboard” styling, decorative gradients, excessive rounded
  cards, or repeated headings.
- Spammy Hermes icons after every tool event.
- Bloated terminal/tool bubbles or raw implementation detail dominating chat.
- Persistent notification noise when no user attention is required.
- Motion on frequently used navigation or decorative animation that makes the
  app feel slower.
